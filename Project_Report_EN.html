Problem Definition and Objectives

The rapid integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into Civil Engineering has grown exponentially, creating a vast but fragmented body of literature. This project addresses the challenge of synthesizing this knowledge by conducting a large-scale, automated systematic literature review. We analyzed 505 academic articles published between 2015 and 2025 to map the intersection of these two fields.

Unlike traditional manual reviews, this study utilizes advanced text mining and natural language processing to objectively classify research trends across multiple dimensions.

Key Project Objectives:

  • Construct a Unified Corpus: Aggregate diverse academic sources (arXiv, CORE, Semantic Scholar) into a single, high-quality dataset of AI-Civil Engineering research.
  • Automate Classification: Develop a rule-based taxonomy system to automatically categorize papers by both AI methodology (e.g., Deep Learning, NLP) and Civil Engineering domain (e.g., Structural Health Monitoring, Geotechnical).
  • Visualize Trends: Build an interactive, data-driven dashboard to reveal evolution patterns, "hot" topics, and cross-disciplinary momentum over the last decade.
  • Identify Research Gaps: Systematically detect under-explored intersections between emerging AI capabilities and traditional engineering problems to guide future research.

Conclusions

Summary

This project successfully:

  • Curated a High-Quality Research Corpus: Aggregated and rigorously filtered a dataset of 505 academic articles from major repositories, establishing a reliable baseline for analyzing the intersection of AI and Civil Engineering.
  • Developed an Automated Classification Taxonomy: Designed and implemented a robust, rule-based keyword extraction system to systematically categorize research papers by Civil Engineering domain, AI methodology, and specific algorithms.
  • Built an Interactive Analytics Dashboard: Engineered and deployed a comprehensive web-based dashboard featuring over 20 advanced visualizations, enabling users to interactively explore data, uncover patterns, and visualize complex relationships.
  • Identified Critical Research Trends and Gaps: Analyzed temporal and thematic data to reveal the explosive growth of Deep Learning, identify high-momentum research areas, and pinpoint significant gaps such as the under-utilization of Robotics in Materials Science.

Contributions

  • A Decade-Spanning Research Corpus (2015-2025): Delivered a structured, machine-readable dataset covering ten years of innovation. This corpus serves as a foundational resource for future bibliometric studies and meta-analyses.
  • Scalable Classification Framework: Established a reusable taxonomy and an extensible Python-based classification pipeline. This framework is designed to be adaptable, facilitating the indexing of future publications or application to other interdisciplinary fields.
  • Open-Source Exploration Tool: Released the visualization dashboard's source code as an open access tool, democratizing advanced bibliometric analysis capabilities for the wider research community and industry practitioners.
  • Strategic Research Roadmap: Provided evidence-based insights that distinguish between saturated topics and high-potential opportunities, offering a data-driven guide for strategic decision-making in research funding and project planning.

Recommendations for Future Improvement

To further elevate the impact and analytical depth of this project, the following enhancements are recommended:

  • Expand Corpus with Additional Sources: Integrate data from premium academic repositories such as IEEE Xplore, ScienceDirect, and Web of Science. A broader dataset will reduce potential source bias and provide a more comprehensive view of global research trends.
  • Implement NLP-Based Semantic Classification: Transition from keyword-based analysis to advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) using models like SciBERT or RoBERTa. This will enable semantic understanding of abstracts, allowing for more accurate categorization of ambiguous terms and the discovery of latent thematic connections.
  • Add Citation Network Analysis: Incorporate citation data to construct a network graph of diverse research papers. This analysis facilitates the identification of seminal works, influential authors, and the intellectual lineage of specific AI methodologies within civil engineering.
  • Develop Predictive Trend Forecasting: Implement machine learning algorithms to analyze historical time-series data. By modelling growth rates and momentum, the system could forecast emerging topics, helping researchers identify high-potential areas before they become saturated.
